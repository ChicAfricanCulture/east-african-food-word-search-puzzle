# East African Food Word Search Puzzle

## A Sankofa Kitchen Cultural Heritage Project

The **East African Food Word Search Puzzle** is part of the Sankofa Kitchen initiative — a digital cultural heritage project dedicated to exploring African foodways beyond the kitchen.

This interactive experience invites participants to slow down, look closely, and learn the names of dishes that carry histories of migration, trade, memory, and everyday life across East Africa.

“Before you cook it, learn to say it.”

---

## Project Purpose

Food is language.
Food is memory.
Food is archive.

This puzzle transforms culinary knowledge into an interactive learning space. As players search for dishes in the grid, they are introduced to pronunciation guidance and short contextual notes through a rotating feature titled:

**Sankofa Kitchen Food Notes**

Each note includes:

```
[Category] Dish — pronunciation — short cultural description
```

The experience is designed to be accessible to all ages while still honoring the cultural depth of the dishes represented.

---

## Cultural Framing

The puzzle includes 30 East African dishes organized into four categories:

* **Street** — everyday foods found in markets and roadside stalls
* **Staples** — foundational foods that anchor daily meals
* **Mains** — stews and central dishes that gather people together
* **Sides** — greens, salads, and accompaniments that complete the plate

Words may appear forward, backward, or diagonally — encouraging focus, patience, and pattern recognition.

As dishes are found:

* They are highlighted in the grid
* Their names are crossed out in the word list
* The rotating notes subtly prioritize unfound dishes, encouraging continued discovery

---

## Educational Intention

This project encourages:

* Pronunciation awareness
* Cultural curiosity
* Intergenerational learning
* Memory strengthening through directional word search
* Recognition of regional food traditions

The goal is not trivia.
The goal is familiarity.

---

## Format & Accessibility

* Single-page interactive design
* 18×18 word grid
* Dark mode option
* Three optional hints
* Pauseable rotating cultural notes
* Reduced motion support for accessibility

The interface remains minimal so that the game itself stays central.
